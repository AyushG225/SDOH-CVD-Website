Most Important Variables Affecting Cardiovascular Disease Death Rates Over Time

    2015

    Description

    This project visualizes the most important social determinants of health affecting cardiovascular disease death rates across U.S. counties over time. By integrating real-world data and predictive forecasts, the map highlights key variables such as years of potential life lost rate, uninsured rates, and more, providing users with a dynamic tool to explore health disparities at the county level.

    Key features of this project include:

    Yearly Data Visualization: Users can explore data across different years, from 2015 to 2023, and examine forecasted trends for 2024 and 2025. The color-coded map provides an intuitive way to understand which social determinants have the most significant impact on cardiovascular health in each county.

    In Depth Unique County Information: Users can click on counties to zoom into a specific county. Once the user clicks into a zoomed county, the website will bring up a county unique page displaying a line chart of death rates (including a forecast), and the most important variables each year.

    Interactive Search and Navigation: The map allows users to search for specific counties by name or FIPS code, providing an easy way to zoom in on areas of interest. The interactive legend helps users filter the map to focus on particular health determinants.

    Forecasting Future Trends: By incorporating forecast data, users can gain insights into how the most critical health determinants are expected to evolve, helping policymakers and health professionals make informed decisions about future interventions.

    This project serves as a valuable tool for public health officials, researchers, and anyone interested in understanding and addressing the factors contributing to cardiovascular disease at the local level.

    Variable Description

    Variable Description
    # associations_per_1000 The number of social, civic, and religious associations per 1,000 population, indicating levels of community engagement and social capital.
    # chlamydia cases_per_1000 The number of reported chlamydia cases per 1,000 population, indicating the prevalence of this sexually transmitted infection.
    # dentists_per_1000 The number of dentists per 1,000 population, indicating the availability of dental care in the community.
    # driving deaths_per_1000 The number of deaths resulting from motor vehicle accidents per 1,000 population.
    # injury deaths_per_1000 The number of deaths due to injuries per 1,000 population, a measure of fatal injuries in the community.
    # primary care physicians_per_1000 The number of primary care physicians per 1,000 population, reflecting access to basic healthcare services.
    # some college_per_1000 The number of people per 1,000 population who have attended some college but have not earned a degree, indicating educational attainment in the community.
    # uninsured_per_1000 The number of people per 1,000 population who lack health insurance, reflecting access to healthcare coverage.
    # unemployed_per_1000 The number of unemployed individuals per 1,000 population, reflecting economic conditions and job availability.
    # workers who drive alone_per_1000 The number of workers per 1,000 population who commute alone by car, indicating commuting patterns and reliance on private vehicles.
    % children in poverty The percentage of children under 18 living in households with incomes below the poverty line, reflecting socioeconomic conditions that can impact health and well-being.
    % long commute - drives alone The percentage of workers who drive alone for 30 minutes or more to work, which can impact physical activity levels and contribute to stress.
    % physically inactive The percentage of adults who report no physical activity during their leisure time, a risk factor for various chronic diseases.
    % severe housing problems The percentage of households facing severe housing issues, including overcrowding, high housing costs relative to income, or lack of basic amenities like plumbing or kitchen facilities.
    % some college The percentage of adults aged 25-44 who have attended some college but have not earned a degree, indicating educational attainment.
    % unemployed The percentage of the labor force that is unemployed, an indicator of economic health and stability.
    % uninsured The percentage of the population without health insurance coverage, which can limit access to healthcare services and contribute to poor health outcomes.
    20th percentile income The income level below which 20% of the population falls, often used to assess the income levels of the lower end of the economic spectrum.
    80th percentile income The income level below which 80% of the population falls, indicating the higher end of the income distribution in a community.
    average daily pm2.5 The average daily concentration of fine particulate matter (PM2.5) in the air, which is linked to respiratory and cardiovascular health issues.
    chlamydia rate The rate of chlamydia infections per population, indicating the prevalence of this common sexually transmitted infection in a community.
    dentist rate The number of dentists per population, reflecting the availability of dental care services in a community.
    food environment index A composite measure of factors that contribute to a healthy food environment, including access to healthy foods and food security.
    income ratio The ratio of income at the 80th percentile to income at the 20th percentile, used to assess income inequality within a population.
    injury death rate The rate of deaths caused by injuries per population, including those from accidents, violence, and other external causes.
    labor force The total number of people aged 16 and over who are either employed or actively seeking employment, reflecting the economic activity of a population.
    population The total number of people living in a given area, used as a basic measure of population size and density.
    teen birth rate The number of live births per 1,000 females aged 15-19, used to assess adolescent reproductive health and related social factors.
    years of potential life lost rate A measure of premature mortality that quantifies the years of life lost due to death before a certain age, often used to assess the impact of early death on a population.
